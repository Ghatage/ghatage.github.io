I had travelled to meet my Aunt for the 4th of July weekend in 2015. It was my first time out. <br>
As soon as the celebrations were over, I decided to head back a day earlier and spend it in New York City.<br>
We've all heard and seen so much about it from the news, TV shows, Movies and Pop culture in general.<br>
THE point of entry to the new world. I had one day, flat sole shows, a back pack and $10 to spare. (Poor grad student and all)<br>
So I head out, on foot, exploring most of midtown by myself. <br>
Here's a brief summary of how that went.<br>

Here's a map of the places I walked to<br>

Walking straight down W 34th Street and a quick side step into W 33rd Street, I found myself across the famous Madison Square Garden. <br>
I took a bunch of pictures here, people were largely uninterested in what anyone else was doing.<br> 
Passed a few indie film makers on the street who were taking random interviews. <br>

Continuing past them I could feel the vibe of the city. It was fast and diverse.<br>
I could swear that in the first 10 minutes I heard at least 6 different languages being spoken. <br>
I walked along with a bunch of artists, I could tell as they were carrying a large canvas and other tools. <br>
We passed from under a scaffolding, as if going through a dark tunnel, we exit it, our eyes took a minute to adjust to the light. <br> 
When it finally did, we saw this beautiful sight. <br>

Since I got a bit thirsty, I bought a bottle of Gatorade from a street vendor. Another shopper there angrily told me to not buy anything from the vendor <br>
As we was selling it at 'holiday prices'. Meh. I was thirsty and needed the energy so I bought it anyway. <br>
Took a few random selfies with few of the smelly superhero actors and head out on W 42st street towards the Avenue of the Americas.<br>
Came across the HBO office where they had setup a window exclusively got Game of Thrones merch! So cool! <br>

My next stops were Grand Central and the Crysler Building when I got a call from a friend, he had seen my post on Facebook and asked me to go visit him. <br>
He was in Lower Manhattan - the Financial District. I was weighing seeing Fifth Ave, and Grand Central vs visiting him. <br>
It was about late afternoon, I figured I should go visit him, so I went underground and spent whatever I had left of the $10 on a subway pass. <br>
After asking a bunch of people for directions, I got on a Yellow line to Lower Manhattan. I was tracking the stops on my phone as the doors open and shut in no time at each stop <br>
When I got off, I headed towards Wall Street. Quick picture with the Raging Bull and then finally made it to my friends place. <br>
